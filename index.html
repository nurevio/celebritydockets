⚖️ Legal Watch

BILLS ON THE HILL

Laws moving through Congress that could change the game for artists, labels, and the entertainment industry.

Congress
NO FAKES Act · AI Deepfake Protection
Bipartisan bill targeting AI-generated replicas of real people’s voice and likeness without consent. Think Drake’s fake Tupac track, Tom Hanks’ unauthorized dental ad. Would give artists a federal right of publicity that survives even after death.
S.1367 · Sens. Coons, Blackburn, Klobuchar, Tillis→
Congress
RAP Act · Lyrics Can’t Be Evidence
The Restoring Artistic Protection Act would block prosecutors from using song lyrics as evidence of guilt. Young Thug’s RICO trial put this in the spotlight — his own verses were read to the jury. Creative expression isn’t a confession.
H.R.4678 · backed by Jay-Z, Killer Mike→
Congress
TICKET Act · Ticketmaster Crackdown
Born from the Taylor Swift Eras Tour meltdown. Would require all-in pricing, ban speculative ticketing, and force platforms to disclose when they’re the seller. Overlaps with the DOJ’s Live Nation antitrust case already on the docket.
S.281 · Senate Commerce Committee→
Congress
Protect Working Musicians · Venue Safety
Post-Astroworld legislation. Would set federal safety standards for live music venues — crowd density limits, emergency exit protocols, medical staff requirements. Travis Scott’s festival crush killed 10 people in 2021. Hundreds of lawsuits followed.
H.R.5576 · House Judiciary→
Congress
Music Fairness Act · Pay Artists on Radio
The US is one of the only countries where AM/FM radio doesn’t pay performers when it plays their songs. Songwriters get paid, performers don’t. This bill would finally fix that. Opposed by the National Association of Broadcasters.
H.R.861 · reintroduced 2025→
