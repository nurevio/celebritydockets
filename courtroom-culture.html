Behind the Bench

Courtroom sketches, courtroom fashion, PR statements on the record, and the theater of celebrity law.

Not every story lives in the filings. What someone wears to their arraignment says as much as the plea they enter. A courtroom sketch can become the image a case is remembered by. The PR statement released ten minutes after a verdict is its own kind of evidence. This is where we track the parts of a celebrity case that don't show up on the docket.

★ Mediation Matchy-Matchy · SDNY · Apr 2026
Lively v. Baldoni - "Twinzies" in Court
Blake Lively and Justin Baldoni showed up to their court-ordered mediation on the 14th floor of a lower Manhattan courthouse - seated in separate rooms with their legal teams - and the internet immediately clocked that they appeared to be color-coordinated. Social media observers dubbed the looks "matchy matchy" and called them "twinzies!" Some theories: "Their stylists are telling them what colors look innocent" · "They asked an AI for fashion advice perhaps." The mediation ended without a settlement. Trial proceeds May 18. ★ Greens & Olives · Federal Court of Australia · Apr 2026
Across the Pond - Wilson and Ghost Both Pick Green
While Blake Lively and Justin Baldoni were running point on the light-pinks-and-olives discourse in lower Manhattan, half a world away the same palette quietly showed up at the Federal Court of Australia. Rebel Wilson entered court in a vivid spring-green blazer over a crisp white shirt; UK producer Amanda Ghost - one of the producers Wilson called out, and who gave evidence the prior week - arrived in a structured olive utility jacket. Day two brought a costume change: Wilson swapped the loose blowout for a slicked-back low ponytail, paired with a white knit. Whether it's stylist coincidence or quietly emerging courtwear theory, green is having a moment on the 2026 defamation circuit. Full docket →

★ THE PR WAR
Lively v. Baldoni · The Lawyers Go at It
DEFENSE · BRYAN FREEDMAN
Freedman for Baldoni / Wayfarer
"This is another desperate attempt by Ms. Lively to 'fix' her negative reputation by misrepresenting and ignoring the evidence."
A masterclass in aggressive defense PR. Freedman teased the release of Baldoni's receipts before filing the $400M countersuit. The strategy worked enough to get most individual defendants dismissed - and arguably to rehabilitate Baldoni's reputation in the court of public opinion.
