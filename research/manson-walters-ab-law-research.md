## The case in one paragraph

Ashley Walters was Marilyn Manson's (Brian Warner's) personal assistant from
**Aug 2010 → Jul 2011** (~11 months). She alleges he lured her into the job
by complimenting her photography, then physically and sexually abused her —
whipped her, threw plates, pinned her down, bit her ear, forced her hand
into his underwear, threatened her. She sued in **Feb 2021** as part of a
coordinated wave of 4 Manson civil suits. ~**10 years** between alleged
abuse and filing.

## The chronological insanity

| Date | What happened |
|---|---|
| Aug 2010 – Jul 2011 | Alleged abuse during her employment |
| **Feb 2021** | First files lawsuit (4-survivor coordinated wave w/ Wood, Bianco, Morgan) |
| Jan 2020 | Phoenix Act (SB 273) had taken effect — but that was criminal-side, didn't help her civil case |
| 2022 | Initial dismissal on SOL grounds |
| 2022 | AB 2777 passes (3-yr civil lookback w/ cover-up requirement) |
| Dec 2023 | Appellate court revives — citing AB 2777 |
| Dec 2025 | Trial court dismisses AGAIN — Walters couldn't meet cover-up requirement |
| **Oct 13, 2025** | AB 250 signed by Newsom — drops cover-up requirement |
| Jan 1, 2026 | AB 250 window opens |
| **Jan 26, 2026** | Judge Steve Cochran revives case under AB 250 — case alive again |

## The legislative pipeline — the real story

Three different California laws. Three different legislators. Same survivor
coalition (Wood / Walters / Bianco / Morgan) showing up to testify each
time. They narrowed the legal escape hatches one by one over 5 years until
they got the exact bill that would unblock their stalled cases.

| Law | Year | Author | Side | What it did |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Phoenix Act** (SB 273) | 2019 signed / 2020 effective | Sen. Susan Rubio (D, San Gabriel Valley) — DV survivor herself · co-auth Asm. Eduardo Garcia | Criminal | Extended criminal DV SOL from 3 → 5 yrs. Wood asked for 10, got 5. |
| **AB 2777** (Sexual Abuse & Cover-up Accountability Act) | 2022 | Asm. Lorena Gonzalez | Civil | 3-yr civil lookback window (Jan 2023–Dec 2026) for adult sexual assault. Required proving institutional cover-up. |
| **AB 250** | 2025 signed / Jan 2026 effective | **Asm. Cecilia Aguiar-Curry** (Assembly Majority Leader) | Civil | Fully retroactive 2-yr window (Jan 2026–Dec 2027). **No cover-up element required.** Plug for AB 2777's gap. |

**Smoking gun timing:** AB 250 was signed Oct 13, 2025 — BEFORE her case got
dismissed in Dec 2025 — meaning the new law was already in the pipe when
the old one failed her. Three weeks after AB 250's window opened (Jan 1),
the judge revived her case (Jan 26). That's coordinated, not coincidental.

## § 47.1 / Lively connection — potential angle

Both California stories are about **survivors using novel state procedure
to fight back or fight back against**:

- **§ 47.1** (used BY Lively/Wayfarer defendants) — anti-retaliation
  procedure that lets a plaintiff like Lively chill defense PR campaigns
- **AB 250** (used BY Walters) — SOL revival that lets stale assault claims
  proceed

These are both California-specific civil-procedure tools that didn't
exist 5 years ago. There's a "California as the legal laboratory" episode
to be made — *how the state is rewriting civil procedure faster than the
federal system can keep up*.

Could pair Walters + Lively + Wayfarer + something like the AB 218 child
sexual assault revival cases. State legislators figuring out that civil
procedure IS the lever when criminal SOL has run out.
