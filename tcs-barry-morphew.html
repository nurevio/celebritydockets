Colorado v.
Barry Morphew

Suzanne Morphew vanished on Mother's Day, May 10, 2020 from her Salida, Colorado home. Her bicycle was found near the road. Her husband Barry was charged in 2021 — then the case was dismissed amid a discovery scandal. Suzanne's remains were recovered in 2023. Barry was re-charged in June 2025. Round two is on the calendar.

★ Citizens-only opinion. Everything here reflects publicly available evidence, court filings, and citizen analysis. Barry Morphew is presumed innocent. Nothing on this page is a legal finding, court verdict, or determination of guilt.

★ Case Overview

Background

Mother's Day, the bike, and a tranquilizer cap.

Suzanne Morphew — 49, mother of two daughters — was last seen alive on the morning of May 10, 2020 at her home in Salida (Chaffee County), Colorado. Later that day, her unattended bicycle and helmet were found near the road, staged in a way Chaffee County investigators called inconsistent with an actual cycling accident.

The investigation surfaced months of marital tension, an extramarital affair on Suzanne's side, and a husband — Barry Morphew — who'd recently purchased BAM (deer tranquilizer) caps. Barry was charged in May 2021 with first-degree murder.

The case collapsed in April 2022: a judge dismissed it without prejudice over repeated discovery sanctions against the prosecution. Suzanne's remains were found in September 2023 in a remote area of Saguache County, Colorado. A BAM cap was recovered near her remains. The Colorado AG's office took over and re-charged Barry in June 2025. Trial is now back on the calendar.

★ Evidence on the Table

State's Theory

The dart cap, the bike, and the affair.

→ BAM tranquilizer cap. Found near Suzanne's remains in 2023. Barry had purchased BAM caps in the months before her disappearance. The state's most powerful piece of physical evidence.

→ The bicycle staging. Investigators say the bike's location and orientation were inconsistent with how a real cycling crash would scatter equipment. Pre-staged scene.

→ The affair Suzanne was reportedly preparing to leave for. Texts. Plans. Her trajectory away from the marriage.

→ Barry's pre-disappearance behavior. What he searched, what he bought, what he said to family in the weeks before May 10.

→ The body recovery + cause-of-death science. "Homicide by undetermined means" + the dart cap places this in the deliberate-killing column.

★ Verdict Watch

Status · Re-charged · Round 2

Round Two.

The Colorado AG's office is running this one. They have what the first prosecution didn't — a body, a dart cap near it, and a clean discovery posture (so far). Barry's defense team will lean heavily on the round-1 dismissal as a blueprint, but the body changes the calculus.

Big open question: can the dart-cap evidence survive a Daubert challenge on chain of custody and the time/distance gap between Barry's purchases and the cap by the remains? That's the case in microcosm.
