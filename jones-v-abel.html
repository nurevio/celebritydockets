Background

Where this case sits today. With Lively v. Wayfarer settled in May 2026, this docket is now the live PR-side accountability proceeding before SDNY.

October 2025 · the original suit. Liman's MTD ruling on Jones's underlying complaint let the defamation and conversion claims survive against the original defendants. Several peripheral counts were trimmed. With both sides' core theories surviving, the case has been in active discovery on parallel tracks.

March 26, 2025 · the counterclaim. Wayfarer Studios LLC and individual co-counterclaimants named in the pleadings filed a defamation counterclaim against Jones and Jonesworks. The counterclaim alleges that Jones made false statements to Blake Lively, Ryan Reynolds, and publicist Leslie Sloane — specifically, that Wayfarer had "orchestrated a smear campaign" against Lively. Jones moved to dismiss. Judge Liman allowed the defamation theory to proceed.

The pleadings describe two parallel campaigns: an alleged smear of Blake Lively (later the subject of Lively v. Wayfarer) and an alleged retaliatory campaign against Jones and Jonesworks for refusing to participate in it. The factual record from this case — text threads, internal communications, vendor decks — became part of the underlying material that informed the Lively litigation.

Stephanie Jones founded Jonesworks LLC, a New York entertainment PR firm. In August 2024, a senior member of the firm exited to start a competing shop and took the Wayfarer Studios account with them. The Christmas Eve 2024 complaint filed by Jones / Jonesworks in New York County Supreme Court — later removed to SDNY as 1:25-cv-00779 — named the departing employee, freelance crisis publicist Melissa Nathan, Justin Baldoni and Wayfarer Studios LLC as defendants on a coordinated-conduct theory.

  • …Second surviving count proceeded alongside the defamation theory per Liman's MTD ruling. Discovery on the counterclaim runs in parallel with the original action.

All counterclaim allegations are disputed. Stephanie Jones / Jonesworks have denied the substance of the counterclaim and continue to litigate the original action.

Original Claims · Jones v. Wayfarer Studios et al.
  • 01Defamation — against the defendants collectively, alleging false statements made to industry contacts and press. Survived MTD · Oct 2025.
  • 02Breach of Fiduciary Duty / Breach of Contract — against the former Jonesworks employee who departed for a competing shop in August 2024.
  • 03Conversion — alleging removal of company property (including a Jonesworks-issued device containing internal communications) on departure. Survived MTD · Oct 2025.
  • 04Tortious Interference — with Jonesworks's existing client relationships and prospective business.
  • 05Civil Conspiracy — alleging coordination across the named defendants.

All allegations in the original action are disputed by the defendants and have not been adjudicated on the merits.

Why It Matters

This is the case behind the case. The text messages, the "social manipulation" decks, the publicist talking points that powered the Lively v. Wayfarer storyline — they ran through the same orbit. Both directions of claims are alive: Wayfarer's defamation counterclaim against Jones (Liman, Mar 2025) AND Jones's core defamation + conversion claims against Wayfarer (Liman, Oct 2025). With Lively v. Wayfarer now settled as of May 2026, this is the only docket left where the PR-side accountability fight is being litigated in open court.
