Jones v. Abel & Wayfarer

Stephanie Jones — founder of Jonesworks — sues her former protégé Jennifer Abel, crisis publicist Melissa Nathan, Justin Baldoni and Wayfarer Studios in SDNY. Filed Christmas Eve 2024. Background

Long before Lively v. Wayfarer exploded into headlines, the people behind the curtain had their own lawsuit. Stephanie Jones ran Jonesworks, the PR shop where Jennifer Abel rose through the ranks before leaving in August 2024 to launch her own firm — RWA Communications — and take Wayfarer's account with her.

On December 24, 2024, Jones filed in SDNY against Abel, freelance crisis publicist Melissa Nathan (of The Agency Group "TAG"), Justin Baldoni and Wayfarer Studios. The complaint paints the picture of a coordinated exit: Abel allegedly siphoning client files, contacts, and a phone full of internal communications on her way out — then collaborating with Nathan and the Wayfarer principals on the alleged smear campaign against Blake Lively that would later become the centerpiece of Lively v. Wayfarer.

Jones says when she pushed back, the same machine turned on her — leaking, defaming, and trying to push her out of her own industry.

In March 2025, Baldoni and Abel countersued Jones, arguing the original complaint itself was retaliatory. In October 2025, Judge Lewis J. Liman issued a key ruling on motions to dismiss — letting Jones's core defamation and conversion claims survive while paring back some peripheral counts. Discovery is ongoing.

Key Claims
  • 01Defamation — Jones alleges Abel, Nathan, Baldoni and Wayfarer made false and damaging statements about her to industry contacts and the press.
  • 02Breach of Fiduciary Duty / Breach of Contract — against Abel, for allegedly violating her duties to Jonesworks while still employed and on her way out.
  • 03Conversion — Jones alleges Abel took company property, including a work phone with internal Jonesworks communications, on her exit.
  • 04Tortious Interference — that the defendants worked together to interfere with Jonesworks's existing client relationships and prospective business.
  • 05Civil Conspiracy — that the smear campaign against Lively and the parallel campaign against Jones were part of a coordinated effort.
Why It Matters

This is the case behind the case. The text messages, the "social manipulation" decks, the publicist talking points that powered the Lively v. Wayfarer storyline — they ran through the same orbit that Jones v. Abel is about. Liman's October 2025 ruling that the defamation and conversion claims could survive a motion to dismiss is what kept the underlying record alive long enough to inform the Lively litigation. With Lively v. Wayfarer now settled as of May 2026, this docket is where the PR-side accountability fight is still very much alive.
