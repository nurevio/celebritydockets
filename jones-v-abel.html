The Christmas Eve 2024 complaint alleged defamation, breach of fiduciary duty, conversion, tortious interference and civil conspiracy — a coordinated smear of Jones and exit-of-Jonesworks-with-the-Wayfarer-account theory. In October 2025, Liman let the core defamation + conversion claims survive the defendants' motion to dismiss. That's what kept the underlying record alive long enough to feed Lively v. Wayfarer.

Background

Long before Lively v. Wayfarer exploded into headlines, the people behind the curtain had their own lawsuit. Stephanie Jones ran Jonesworks, the PR shop where Jennifer Abel rose through the ranks before leaving in August 2024 to launch her own firm — RWA Communications — and take Wayfarer's account with her.

On December 24, 2024, Jones filed in SDNY against Abel, freelance crisis publicist Melissa Nathan (of The Agency Group "TAG"), Justin Baldoni and Wayfarer Studios. The complaint paints the picture of a coordinated exit: Abel allegedly siphoning client files, contacts, and a phone full of internal communications on her way out — then collaborating with Nathan and the Wayfarer principals on the alleged smear campaign against Blake Lively that would later become the centerpiece of Lively v. Wayfarer.

Jones says when she pushed back, the same machine turned on her — leaking, defaming, and trying to push her out of her own industry.

The counterclaim flip · March 2025. Wayfarer Studios + Justin Baldoni + Jennifer Abel filed counterclaims against Jones / Jonesworks alleging that Jones told Blake Lively, Ryan Reynolds, and publicist Leslie Sloane that Wayfarer had "orchestrated a smear campaign" against Lively. Jones moved to dismiss. Judge Liman ruled in March 2025 that two counterclaims — including the defamation theory — could move forward. That's why this case caption is now also Wayfarer v. Jones.

Liman's October 2025 ruling on Jones's original claims (the inside-the-card suit) similarly let the core defamation and conversion theories proceed. So both sides are now in discovery, both sides have surviving claims, and both sides have something at stake in the same docket. With Lively v. Wayfarer now SETTLED as of May 2026, this is where the PR-side accountability fight is still very much alive.

  • CC2Second counterclaim allowed to proceed by Liman alongside the defamation theory.
Original Claims · Jones v. Abel & Wayfarer (inside the card)
  • 01Defamation — Jones alleges Abel, Nathan, Baldoni and Wayfarer made false and damaging statements about her to industry contacts and the press. Survived MTD · Oct 2025.
  • 02Breach of Fiduciary Duty / Breach of Contract — against Abel, for allegedly violating her duties to Jonesworks while still employed and on her way out.
  • 03Conversion — Jones alleges Abel took company property, including a work phone with internal Jonesworks communications, on her exit. Survived MTD · Oct 2025.
  • 04Tortious Interference — that the defendants worked together to interfere with Jonesworks's existing client relationships and prospective business.
  • 05Civil Conspiracy — that the smear campaign against Lively and the parallel campaign against Jones were part of a coordinated effort.
Why It Matters

This is the case behind the case. The text messages, the "social manipulation" decks, the publicist talking points that powered the Lively v. Wayfarer storyline — they ran through the same orbit. Both directions of claims are alive: Wayfarer's defamation counterclaim against Jones (Liman, Mar 2025) AND Jones's core defamation + conversion claims against Wayfarer (Liman, Oct 2025). With Lively v. Wayfarer now settled as of May 2026, this is the only docket left where the PR-side accountability fight is being litigated in open court.
