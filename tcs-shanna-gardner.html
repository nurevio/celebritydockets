Florida v. Shanna Gardner-Fernandez

On February 16, 2022, Microsoft Bing executive Jared Bridegan was ambushed and shot dead in front of his 2-year-old daughter on a Jacksonville Beach side street. The state says his ex-wife — mother of two of his older children — orchestrated it. The custody fight that turned into a murder-for-hire.

★ Citizen-investigation page. All allegations on this wall are pulled from publicly available filings, charging documents and on-the-record reporting. Shanna Gardner-Fernandez is presumed innocent. Nothing here is a legal finding or determination of guilt.

★ Case Overview

Background

Tire spike. Side street. Toddler in the back seat.

Jared Bridegan — 33, executive at Microsoft Bing — was driving home with his 2-year-old daughter Bexley after dropping his older twin children at their mother's house. On a quiet Jacksonville Beach side street, his SUV's tire was hit by a spike strip placed in the road. He stopped to investigate. He was shot multiple times. Bexley was unharmed in the car seat. Jared died at the scene.

Investigators traced the conspiracy back through paid intermediaries to Shanna Gardner-Fernandez — Jared's ex-wife, mother of his older twins, and a longtime opponent in a contentious custody dispute. She was arrested and charged in March 2023, more than a year after the murder. Her current husband Mario Fernandez Saldana was charged alongside her. Hitman Henry Tenon pleaded guilty in 2023 and is serving life. A fourth alleged co-conspirator was charged with conspiracy.

Shanna remains in custody. Trial pending — multiple delays, motions in limine, gag-order discussions ongoing.

★ Evidence on the Table

State's Theory

Money. Motive. The custody file.

→ The custody battle. Years of escalating disputes between Jared and Shanna over their twins. Court filings. Restraining-order requests. Texts.

→ Cash trail. Allegations of payments routed through Mario to Tenon to fund the hit.

→ Tenon's cooperation. The shooter pleaded guilty and is now a state witness. His version of the chain of command is the spine of the prosecution case.

→ The duplex. Tenon and Mario lived in the same building. Geography that the state says is not a coincidence.

→ Communications. Texts, emails, and burner-phone records the state says tie Shanna to the planning conversations.

★ Theories on the Wall

Theory · 01Custody fight that "kept escalating" — the paper trail of family-court grievances reads like a motive board on its own.
Theory · 02The spike strip placement. Pre-meditated geography — that side street was chosen for a reason.
Theory · 03Tenon flipped early and hard. The state's whole case may live or die on his credibility on cross.

★ Verdict Watch

Status · Trial Pending

Awaiting trial.

Shanna remains in custody. Mario remains in custody. Tenon is locked up with his cooperation deal. The state is moving toward a jury trial — severance question still on the table for Shanna v. Mario, plus a steady drumbeat of pretrial motions on admissibility.

Big open questions for the jury, when they finally land: does Shanna's voice ever appear directly in any of the planning communications? And can Tenon's testimony survive the credibility cross? If yes to both, this is a long sentence. If no, the conspiracy theory has to do all the lifting on its own.
