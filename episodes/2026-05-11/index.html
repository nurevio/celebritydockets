Story 03 · The Verdict
Dedham District Court, MA · ✅ NOT GUILTY · May 5, 2026

Commonwealth v. Diggs · NOT GUILTY

After a two-day trial, Stefon Diggs walked. Less than two hours of deliberation. Acquitted on felony strangulation AND misdemeanor A&B. We walk through Day 1 (Mila Adams direct), Day 2 (the cross, the $5.5M demand reveal, defense witnesses and Kettlewell's closing), and the post-verdict statement from Mitch Schuster.

★ how the trial actually went · day 1 → day 2

Two days. Seven defense witnesses. Less than 2 hours.

Day 1 · Mon May 4

Mila Adams · direct.

The chef takes the stand. Says Diggs slapped her across the face and tried to choke her with the crux of his elbow after a December 2025 argument over pay.

Prosecution sets up the timeline. No medical records, no photos.

Day 2 · Tues May 5

The cross. The defense. The verdict.

Sara Silva on cross asks Adams whether her lawyer demanded $5.5M from Diggs. Adams replies that Diggs offered her $100K to recant. Judge tells the jury to disregard.

Defense calls all seven of its witnesses — chief of staff, massage therapist, nurse, hair stylist among them. Prosecution rests. Andrew Kettlewell closes first: no medical records, no photos, no witness testimony of an assault.

Jury out at 2 PM closings. Back in under 2 hours. NOT GUILTY on both counts.

★ defense statement · @conlin_lauren

Schuster: "this case never should have gone to trial."

Defense attorney Mitch Schuster outside the courthouse, framing the verdict as a warning about how quickly civil settlement demands can ride alongside criminal complaints when an athlete is the target.

★ mitch schuster · diggs counsel

"We have taken these allegations seriously from day one and that's exactly why we were eager for the facts to come to light through the legal process."

"The evidence has shown what we've maintained from day one: Mr. Diggs was wrongly accused, and this case represents exactly the kind of opportunistic targeting that players can face the moment they step off the field."

"People have to stop targeting professional athletes and trying to extract money. They have a target on their back and it's hard enough doing what they do. This has to stop."

★ rundown · kilcher v. cameron · c.d. cal. · may 5

Avatar's Neytiri... built from a 14-year-old's face?

Brand-new federal complaint filed May 5, 2026 in the Central District of California (2:26-cv-04832). Q'orianka Kilcher — the indigenous actor who played Pocahontas at age 14 in The New World, later starred in Yellowstone — sues James Cameron and The Walt Disney Co., alleging Cameron "extracted her facial features" from a still of The New World and "directed his design team to use it as the foundation for the character of Neytiri."

★ The pipeline · what the complaint alleges

Photo → production sketches → sculpted 3D maquettes → laser-scanned digital models → distributed to multiple VFX vendors → rendered into Neytiri (performed by Zoe Saldaña).

Two big legal theories:

  • Right of publicity · classic Cal. Civ. Code §3344 misappropriation.
  • California deepfake statute · minor's likeness used in a digital model later rendered into adult intimate scenes — novel application.

Avatar grosses ~$3B. Kilcher seeks disgorgement of profits attributable to her likeness, plus compensatory + punitive damages, injunction, and corrective public disclosure.

★ what a settlement looks like · for the lively v. wayfarer wait

When the joint statement says — what's actually on the docket?

We're still waiting on the Lively v. Wayfarer SDNY settlement papers to hit the docket. While we wait, here's a useful side-by-side from the Busta Rhymes case — this is exactly what a federal-court settlement notice looks like in the wild.

apr 29, 2026 · doc 28

The joint status letter.

"The parties submit this Joint Status Letter pursuant to the Court's February 17, 2026 Order. On April 22, 2026, the parties settled this matter in private mediation before Hon. Steven M. Gold (Ret.)…"

apr 30, 2026 · order

The court's response.

"By 06/01/2026, the parties shall file their motion for settlement agreement pursuant to Cheeks v. Freeport Pancake House…" Mag. J. Henry's order requires (1) executed agreement (2) Wolinsky letter brief (3) damages calc (4) attorney fees + billing.

FLSA cases need court approval per Cheeks. Lively v. Wayfarer isn't FLSA — but the joint-status-letter format on the docket will look almost identical when it lands.
