Story 01 · Lively v. Wayfarer

The day after.

Judge Liman heard oral argument Sunday. Now we wait. Meanwhile McSweeney's already citing his ruling in her own SDNY case. Same judge. Same legal question about where defense crosses into retaliation.

★ what's pending · how this lands

No right of appeal.

The Stakes

If Lively wins on § 47.1: attorneys' fees + auto-tripled compensatory + punitive damages. If she loses: nothing. No appeal. Whatever Liman decides — that's it.

The Ripple — McSweeney

RHONY's Leah McSweeney's May 26 opposition to Cohen's MTD cites Lively v. Wayfarer TWICE. Quotes Liman directly. Same judge. Same SDNY court. Same question: where does defense cross into retaliation?

"There comes a point where the accused stops simply defending him or herself and starts taking action that a reasonable jury could view as retaliation."

— Judge Lewis J. Liman, Lively v. Wayfarer Studios LLC, 2026 WL 905447

Story 02 · Walters v. Warner

Manson loses again.

California judge refused to toss or trim Ashley Walters' Third Amended Complaint. Five months after AB 250 revived the case, Manson's MTD fails again. Case moves forward — summary judgment after discovery.

★ may 2026 · 3rd amended complaint · ab 250 lookback

What survives.

The Allegations

Walters alleges Manson (Brian Warner) pushed her onto a bed, pinned her arms, and tried to kiss her at his West Hollywood home studio in May 2010. From her time as his personal assistant.

The Law

AB 250 gives older sexual-assault claims a 2-year lookback window. Case dismissed Dec 2025 → revived Jan 26 → MTD denied again now. Manson can still try summary judgment after discovery.

Story 03 · Flagg v. Swift

33 pages. Two tracks.

Swift's legal team dropped a motion to dismiss the day before the PI hearing — and Flagg's lawyer called them out for violating the meet-and-confer rule. PI ruling pending. MTD hearing August 5. Plus the Gaga Mayhem precedent her own lawyer cited.

★ swift's mtd · two-pronged attack

The 33-page bombshell.

Failure to State a Claim

"Shotgun pleading" lumps Swift, TASRM, UMG, Bravado together 90+ times. No likelihood of confusion — completely different goods. USPTO refusal was a non-final office action.

No Personal Jurisdiction

Swift domiciled in Tennessee (sworn declaration). TASRM is a TN LLC. Property in CA ≠ general jurisdiction. Calder effects test not met.

Flagg's counsel filed a noncompliance notice — Swift's team violated Local Rule 7-3 (meet-and-confer) by filing 3 days too early.

★ the precedent · gaga mayhem

Gaga did it first.

Swift's lawyer cited Lost International v. Germanotta at the PI hearing — a judge denied an injunction against Gaga using "Mayhem" for her album and merch against a surf company's claims. Swift's team said it's "on all fours" with this one.
