Story 02 · Flagg v. Swift

33 pages. Two tracks.

Swift's legal team dropped a motion to dismiss the day before the PI hearing — and Flagg's lawyer called them out for violating the meet-and-confer rule. PI ruling pending. MTD hearing August 5. Plus the Gaga Mayhem precedent her own lawyer cited.

★ swift's mtd · two-pronged attack

The 33-page bombshell.

Failure to State a Claim

"Shotgun pleading" lumps Swift, TASRM, UMG, Bravado together 90+ times. No likelihood of confusion — completely different goods. USPTO refusal was a non-final office action.

No Personal Jurisdiction

Swift domiciled in Tennessee (sworn declaration). TASRM is a TN LLC. Property in CA ≠ general jurisdiction. Calder effects test not met.

Flagg's counsel filed a noncompliance notice — Swift's team violated Local Rule 7-3 (meet-and-confer) by filing 3 days too early.

★ the precedent · gaga mayhem

Gaga did it first.

Swift's lawyer cited Lost International v. Germanotta at the PI hearing — a judge denied an injunction against Gaga using "Mayhem" for her album and merch against a surf company's claims. Swift's team said it's "on all fours" with this one.

★ Story 02b · The Madison Square Garden Twist

She’s getting married at The Garden.

TMZ + Page Six broke it Jun 5: Taylor + Travis Kelce booking Madison Square Garden for a July 3 wedding. 1,100–1,200 guests. Text invites only. No physical paper. NYPD coordinating street closures. The venue was chosen because no windows = no photographers.

★ The Echo · February vs. June

Wait — who else said Madison Square Garden lately?

Four months ago, on Levin & Geragos’ 2 Angry Men podcast, Bryan Freedman — Justin Baldoni’s attorney — said this about Blake Lively’s deposition:

“You could sell tickets at Madison Square Garden for that.”

— Bryan Freedman · 2 Angry Men podcast · Feb 2026

In the same window, Freedman also subpoenaed Taylor Swift in the Lively/Baldoni case — arguing her involvement in the film could be deposition material. Swift’s rep called it “tabloid clickbait masquerading as legal strategy.”

Now Swift’s booking that exact building for the most photographed event of 2026.

“Madison Square Garden was chosen because there are no windows from which photographers can shoot pictures.”

— TMZ · reporting the wedding venue rationale

Coincidence? Maybe. But every search for “Taylor Swift Madison Square Garden” from now until the wedding will surface Freedman’s “sell tickets” quote in the same SEO neighborhood. The Garden is the new battleground.

★ how the press is framing it

“Devastating Blake Lively diss.”

That’s Cinemablend’s headline. The read: Lively allegedly pressured Swift for public support during the Wayfarer mess — Swift declined. Now Swift’s wedding venue puts her geographically + symbolically in the same building Freedman name-checked while subpoenaing her.

Team Swift Read

Privacy choice. No windows. Underground parking. Avoiding paparazzi after a year of being dragged through litigation she didn’t ask to be part of.

Team Lively Read

Choosing the venue Freedman publicly invoked is petty signaling. A reminder of which side Swift quietly chose by NOT defending Lively when subpoenaed.

Neither Swift nor Kelce have publicly confirmed the venue. Reps did not respond to multiple outlets. Take TMZ + Page Six with caveat — but the date (Jul 3) and headcount (1,100+) is now repeated across 8+ outlets.
